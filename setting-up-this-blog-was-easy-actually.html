setting up this blog was easy, actually

katrin Aug 31, 2025

this is not a glorious post. i do have a computer science bachelors. however, i did suffer massively from the paradox of choice.

this sunday i thought its time for a blog. i gave twitter too many of my words already. i twote like ~200.000 posts over a 8-year span. and tumblr was too messy for me to blog on (also, i am scared of the community).

the process, the tools

  1. google “how to microblog”. be overwhelmed. too many words.
  2. type “host own blog” into youtube. hope someone says something along the lines of “github pages”.
  3. watch a video of a guy massively overengineering his notes into a website on github pages (half victory)
  4. find the holy grail video of a guy (after many lectures and hours of programming uni, i understood how a website works?)
  5. download publii
  6. set up pages
  7. set up cloudflare protection for my domain
  8. set up subdomain through CF
  9. … it’s … a blog?

what i learned

a website does not need much. it is a domain, a hosting provider and a CMS. a domain can be free (github pages default or neocities), hosting too (see above), and the CMS can also be free (thanks publii).

and emotionally? i learned it does not need to be hard. computer scientists tend to flex with the state-of-the-art tools and security features they used to overcomplicate a simple task – i am not those kinds of people, i am initimidated my big words.
